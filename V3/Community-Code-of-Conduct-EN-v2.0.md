# 1. Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

# 2. Our Core Values

To build a sustainable and healthy community, we adhere to five core values that guide our interactions:

## **Mutual Respect**
We respect the dignity, opinions, and backgrounds of every individual, even when we disagree. We listen actively, avoid mockery or belittling, and demonstrate genuine understanding for others' perspectives and experiences. Respect means acknowledging different communication styles and making conscious efforts to include people who differ from us.

## **Lead with Empathy**
We listen with purpose, seeking to understand before being understood. We conduct ourselves professionally while showing kindness and compassion. We recognize that people make mistakes and deserve support in correcting them. We assume good intent and interpret others' statements in good faith, while remaining aware of our impact on others' emotions and actions.

## **Transparency**
We believe in open communication regarding decisions and actions, while maintaining necessary confidentiality for sensitive issues. We clarify our intentions, decisions, and conflicts of interest clearly and honestly. We communicate productively and recognize that clear communication requires conscious effort.

## **Accountability**
Every member, from the President to the newest recruit, is responsible for their actions and their impact on the community. We take responsibility for our mistakes, apologize to those affected, and learn from the experience. We commit to delivering on our promises, maintaining high-quality work, and respecting the time and effort of our peers.

## **Inclusion & Belonging**
We actively work to remove barriers and ensure that everyone has equal access to opportunities and feels a sense of belonging. We celebrate diversity, promote welcoming environments, and work to understand the experiences of people different from ourselves. We make inclusive choices in our interactions and leadership.

# 3. Our Standards

## Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment:

- Demonstrating empathy and kindness toward other people
- Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Accepting responsibility and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
- Focusing on what is best not just for us as individuals, but for the overall community
- Making a conscious effort to include people who differ from you
- Asking questions to understand rather than to judge
- Showing willingness to understand others' situations and emotions
- Fostering environments that allow all members to feel connected

## Examples of Unacceptable Behavior:

### **Category A: Zero Tolerance Violations (Severe Misconduct)**

**Harassment and Violence:**
- Any form of verbal, physical, or online harassment
- Threats or intimidation of violence
- Violent or aggressive behavior
- Sustained inappropriate behavior toward an individual
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment

**Sexual Harassment (Severe):**
- Unwelcome sexual advances of any kind
- Sexual comments or inappropriate gender-based jokes
- Excessive, unwelcome romantic attention
- Unwelcome physical contact of a sexual nature
- Conditioning a benefit (e.g., promotion, task assignment) in exchange for romantic or sexual favors (Quid Pro Quo)
- Sharing or displaying sexually explicit content
- Using sexually degrading words

**Discrimination:**
- Harassment or discrimination based on age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual orientation
- Using discriminatory, racist, or religious hate language
- Intentional discriminatory behavior
- Exclusion based on identity characteristics

**Theft and Betrayal:**
- Theft of community resources, equipment, or funds
- Betrayal of trust through unauthorized disclosure of confidential information
- Fraud or falsification of records
- Exploitation of position for personal gain
- Plagiarism or intellectual property theft

**Doxing:**
- Publishing others' private information (physical address, email, phone number) without explicit permission
- Revealing real identity without consent
- Sharing private photos or data
- Exposing sensitive personal information

**Consequences:** Immediate removal from community and permanent ban.

---

### **Category B: Serious Infractions (Professional Misconduct)**

**Sustained Inappropriate Behavior:**
- Repeated instances of disrespectful communication
- Persistent dishonesty (within a week)
- Repeated mockery or belittling of others
- Emotional pressure, manipulation, or control tactics
- Accumulated violations of Category C over time

**Non-Compliance with Standards:**
- Persistent absence without valid excuse
- Repeated failure to deliver on commitments (multiple instances)
- Consistently poor quality work
- Lack of genuine engagement or participation
- Non-compliance with Code of Conduct or leadership decisions
- Publishing inappropriate content or conducting activities misaligned with community values

**Consequences:** Formal warning, probation period, or temporary suspension (1-4 weeks).

---

### **Category C: Minor Infractions (Operational)**

- Late task delivery (less than 24 hours) without prior notice
- Missing a regular meeting without excuse
- Minor disrespectful tone in communication
- "Ghosting" (no response) for 24 hours on urgent tasks
- Dismissing or mocking beginners' questions (e.g., "feigned surprise" or "well-actuallys")
- Single instance of unprofessional behavior

**Consequences:** Verbal warning or friendly reminder (no formal documentation).

# 4. Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

---

# 5. Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces (online and offline), and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event.

# 6. Enforcement & Investigation Protocol

## Investigation Timeline (Defined & Transparent)

All reports will be handled according to the following timeline to ensure swift and fair resolution:

### **Day 1 (24 hours):**
- Incident report received
- Immediate acknowledgment from leadership
- Preliminary assessment of severity
- Notification to involved parties of investigation initiation

### **Days 2-3 (48-72 hours):**
- Comprehensive investigation begins
- Interviews with reporter, respondent, and witnesses
- Collection of evidence and documentation
- Initial assessment of violation category

### **Days 4-5 (96-120 hours):**
- Completion of investigation
- Documentation of all findings
- Assessment of community impact
- Preparation for determination meeting

### **Days 6-7:**
- Leadership review and secret ballot voting
- Final determination of consequences
- Documentation of decision and rationale

### **Days 8-10:**
- Notification of all parties with decision
- Explanation of consequences and reasoning
- Appeals timeline communicated
- Final documentation and file storage

**Maximum Duration:** 10 days from initial report to final determination.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines & Consequences

### **Level 1: Verbal Warning (Correction)**

**When Applied:** Category C violations or first offense

**Process:**
1. Private conversation with community leader
2. Clear explanation of behavior and impact
3. Opportunity for immediate correction
4. No formal documentation (informal note only)
5. 2-day timeframe to demonstrate improvement

**Outcome:**
- If improved: Matter closed ‚úÖ
- If repeated: Escalate to written warning ‚ö†Ô∏è

---

### **Level 2: Written Warning**

**When Applied:** Repeated Category C violations or Category B violations (first instance)

**Process:**
1. Formal meeting with Head and Vice President
2. Documentation of all facts and evidence
3. Clear explanation of violation
4. Opportunity for response and defense
5. Signed written warning placed in file
6. Probation period: 1-4 weeks (depending on severity)

**Restrictions During Probation:**
- Close monitoring of behavior
- Possible removal from certain responsibilities
- Weekly check-ins with leadership
- No advancement or leadership opportunities

**Outcome:**
- If improvement shown: Warning remains in file (can be cleared after 30 days of good conduct) ‚úÖ
- If violation repeated: Escalate to temporary suspension ‚ö†Ô∏è

---

### **Level 3: Temporary Suspension**

**When Applied:** Failure to improve after written warning or serious Category B violations

**Duration:** 1-4 weeks (depending on severity)

**Process:**
1. Emergency leadership meeting
2. Formal meeting with respondent
3. Detailed explanation of suspension and duration
4. Right to full defense
5. Written notice of suspension
6. Immediate removal from:
   - Leadership roles
   - Current projects
   - Community meetings and discussions
   - Decision-making authority

**During Suspension:**
- Weekly check-in meetings with Vice President
- Assessment of progress toward improvement
- Opportunity to demonstrate behavioral change
- Community remains protected from further incidents

**Return After Suspension:**
- Meeting with Vice President for evaluation
- Assessment of genuine improvement
- Commitment to follow Code of Conduct
- Gradual reinstatement to responsibilities
- **OR** recommendation for permanent removal

---

### **Level 4: Permanent Removal (Immediate)**

**When Applied:** Category A violations (zero tolerance)

**Process:**

**Phase 1: Preparation (24-48 hours)**
- Complete evidence and witness collection
- Full file review
- Documentation of all facts
- Respondent notified of hearing

**Phase 2: Formal Hearing (1-2 hours)**
- Meeting with President and High Board
- Respondent present (right to full defense)
- Presentation of evidence
- Opportunity for respondent to respond
- Questions and clarifications
- Discussion of community impact

**Phase 3: Secret Ballot Vote**
- Secret voting (no one knows how others voted)
- Complete neutrality required (conflict of interest check)
- Consensus required or majority of 2/3
- Documentation of voting rationale
- Written decision issued

**Phase 4: Implementation**
- Immediate formal notice of removal
- Removal from all roles
- Deletion from all groups and channels
- Ban from all community events and activities
- No future participation without special approval

**Phase 5: Final Documentation**
- Complete file archived
- Comprehensive documentation
- Privacy protection maintained
- No disclosure except by legal requirement

**Permanent Ban Status:**
- Immediate and indefinite removal from community
- No attendance at events
- No participation in discussions
- No leadership opportunities
- No reinstatement unless approved by unanimous High Board vote after minimum 1 year

**Possible Reinstatement:** After 1 year minimum
- Formal written apology required
- Unanimous approval from President and High Board
- Probationary period of 3 months
- Starting as new member (no previous privileges)

# 7. Operational Implementation & Strike System

## Classification of Violations

### **Category C (Minor Infractions - Operational)**
- Corresponds to "Level 1: Correction"
- Late task delivery (< 24 hours) without notice
- Missing meeting without excuse
- Minor disrespectful tone
- 24-hour "ghosting" on urgent tasks
- **Penalty:** 1 Strike

### **Category B (Serious Infractions - Professional)**
- Corresponds to "Level 2: Warning" or "Level 3: Temporary Ban"
- Accumulation of 3 Strikes
- Complete failure to deliver major project
- 3+ days of "ghosting"
- Creating hostile environment
- **Penalty:** Formal Warning or Probation

### **Category A (Zero Tolerance - Ethical/Legal)**
- Corresponds to "Level 4: Permanent Ban"
- Harassment (sexual/verbal)
- Discrimination
- Theft of funds/intellectual property
- Violence or credible threats
- Doxing
- Quid Pro Quo violations
- **Penalty:** Immediate Removal (10-day investigation)

---

## The Strike & Warning Mechanism

**Accumulation Rule:**
- **3 Strikes = 1 Formal Warning**
- **3 Warnings = Removal from Community**

**The "Reset" Rule (Inclusion Policy):**
- If a member maintains a clean record for **30 days** after a Strike, one Strike will be automatically removed
- Allows for redemption and second chances
- Encourages positive behavioral change

**The "Redemption" Rule (Active Path to Recovery):**
- A member with a Strike can volunteer for a proactive "Redemption Task":
  - Delivering high-quality work early
  - Helping other community members
  - Taking initiative on community improvements
- Immediate Strike removal upon completion, subject to leadership approval
- Demonstrates commitment to community values

# 8. Conflict of Interest & Recusal Policy

To ensure fair judgment and eliminate bias, any community leader who:
- Has a close personal relationship with either party
- Is directly involved in the incident
- Has a conflict of interest in the matter
- Cannot remain objective

**Must immediately RECUSE themselves** from:
- Investigation
- Evidence review
- Decision-making
- Voting

The case will be handed over to:
- A neutral leader not involved
- An ad-hoc committee of unbiased members
- External mediator if necessary

This ensures every decision is made by people without personal stakes or bias.

# 9. Privacy & Confidentiality

We value your privacy and the security of all involved parties.

## Confidentiality Measures:

- All reports kept confidential to the maximum extent possible
- Details shared only with leaders directly involved in investigation
- Reporter's identity protected from respondent (except when necessary)
- No public naming of involved parties without explicit consent
- No disclosure except as required by law or institutional policy
- Incident details not shared on community channels

## Reporter Protection:

- Complete protection from retaliation
- No discrimination or adverse treatment
- Confidential reporting channels available
- Anonymous reporting option
- Support resources provided

## Record Management:

- Secure file storage
- Limited access (leadership only)
- Time-based retention policy
- Destruction of files per policy
- Protection of sensitive information

# 10. Appeals Process

Any member who believes they have been unfairly penalized has the right to appeal their case.

### **Step 1: Submission (Within 7 Days)**

Submit a written appeal to the High Board including:
- Specific decision being appealed
- Clear reasons for appeal
- New evidence (if any)
- Your perspective on the case
- Requested outcome

### **Step 2: Review Assignment (Within 2 Days)**

- Neutral party assigned (not involved in original decision)
- Different Head or High Board member
- Verification of conflict-free status
- Acknowledgment sent to appellant

### **Step 3: Re-Hearing (Within 5 Days)**

- Review of original evidence
- Consideration of new evidence
- Private meeting with appellant
- Full opportunity to present your case
- Questions and clarifications allowed
- Fair reassessment of circumstances

### **Step 4: Final Decision (Within 10 Days)**

Possible outcomes:
- **Uphold:** Original decision stands
- **Reduce:** Penalty lessened based on new evidence
- **Overturn:** Decision reversed

This decision is **final and binding**.
